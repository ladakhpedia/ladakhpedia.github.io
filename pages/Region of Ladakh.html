Regions of Ladakh

Ladakh ("Land of High Passes") is an administrative and culturally distinct region of India located in the eastern part of Jammu and Kashmir State. It is divided into two Districts, Kargil and Leh. The northernmost part of India, Ladakh borders Pakistan to the north (the Karakoram Mountains serving as the boundary)and China to the east (on the Tibetan Plateau) with the Great Himalayan Range creating its southern border to the rest of India.

It is one of the most sparsely populated regions of the country, inhabited by people of Indo-Aryan and Tibetan descent, and it a land of stark beauty with its high, snow-capped mountains, village oases, and rare and endangered wildlife.

File:Regions of Ladakh.jpg

Ladakh was divided into two districts in 1979[1] with Kargil and Leh serving as the District Seats. Administratively, Leh is divided by 6 Kargil by 4 Sub-Divisions. But whole Ladakh can roughly be divided into 8 areas, by local culture, geography and language differences.

Leh and Surrounding Area

Leh serves as the district seat of Leh District and is the largest town in the region.

Shaam Region

Shaam (Lower Ladakh) refers to the large area downstream along the Indus from Leh, and extending south to the border with Zanskar and west to the border with Kargil District.

Changthang Region

Changthang (Northern Plateau) is the north and eastern part of Ladakh that sits on the Tibetan Plateau, with sparsely-populated villages largely above 4000m altitude and most inhabitants dependent on their herds of yaks, sheep and goats.

Nubra Region

Nubra is a three-legged valley lying to the north of the Leh / Indus Valley and separates the Ladakh and Karakoram Mountain Ranges. It is famed as the Valley of Flowers (where its name originates) and the two-humped Bactrian camels.

Kargil and Surrounding Area

Kargil City is the second largest in Ladakh after Leh, situated at 2,676 meters along the banks of the Suru River.

Northwestern Kargil

To the northwest of Kargil, the land rises steeply into the Great Himalayan Range with some of the coldest villages in the region. In this area, a single raod connects Ladakh to Jammu and Kashmir State.

The lush Suru Valley runs through the heart of southern Kargil where plentiful water supports the richest agriculture in the region.

Zanskar Region

Bordering Himachal Pradesh along the Great Himalayan Mountains, Zanskar is the most remote region in Ladakh, connected to Kargil by the tenuous Pensi La pass and to Leh District in winter along the frozen Zanskar River.
